Race

Hate crimes and bias incidents are on the rise on college campuses. For years, racial bias has been the most common motivation for committing such crimes.Despite the abundance of evidence demonstrating that hate crimes and bias incidents are on the rise, limited reporting options suggest that many of these actions could go unrecorded.

Gender and Sexual Orientation

Despite the fact that Lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer (LGBTQ) students are more visible than ever on U.S. college campuses, problems still remain. Three core issues are 1) identity development, 2) campus climate, and 3) state and national social and policy contexts.
Click here to learn more about discrimination on the basis of sexual and gender orientation specifically on college campuses.


Sexual Assault

Despite active efforts to put an end to sexual assault and misconduct on college campuses, these issues are still extremely prominent. Women are at least three times more likely to experience sexual assualt, and according to the Department of Justice, 80% of sexual violence cases go unreported. Women tend to avoid filing reports out of fear or embarrassment, coupled with the notion that police cannot do anything to help.
